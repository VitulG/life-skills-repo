# Growth Mindset Introduction: What it is, How it Works, and Why it Matters by Trevor Ragan

Growth mindset means believing that your abilities and intelligence can improve with effort, learning and persistence; by embracing challenges, 
mistakes and feedback, you unlock real growth instead of being limited by a fixed idea of “talent.”

# What is Internal Locus of Control?

1. Locus of Control is a psychological concept referring to how much we believe we control the events in our life vs. how much is controlled by outside forces.
2. Someone with an internal locus of control believes that outcomes success or failure largely stem from their own actions, decisions, effort, and attitude.
3. In contrast, in an external locus of control, a person tends to attribute outcomes to external factors like luck, fate, other people, environment, or chance.
4. Internal locus means I can influence what happens to me” rather than “things happen to me

# Key Points of this video

Sustained motivation comes from believing you have control over your life and its outcomes by cultivating an internal locus of control, you become more responsible 
for your actions and more committed to effort.

# How to Build a Growth Mindset?

1. Believe you can improve
2. Embrace challenges
3. Learn from failures instead of avoiding them
4. Stay consistent with practice and effort
5. Seek feedback regularly
6. Focus on progress, not perfection
7. Surround yourself with growth-oriented people
8. Celebrate small wins
9. Stay curious and keep learning
10. Maintain positive self-talk and supportive inner dialogue

# Ideas to take action and build Growth Mindset

1. Take full responsibility for your learning no excuses.
2. Stick with a problem until it is fully solved; no quitting midway.
3. Always put in more effort when you don’t understand something.
4. Never write code you don’t understand pause and learn first.
5. Before moving ahead, make sure each concept becomes crystal clear.
6. Treat every new topic or difficulty as a chance to grow.
7. Stay relaxed and focused even when things break or go wrong.
8. Use documentation, Google, Stack Overflow, GitHub Issues, and the internet before asking for help.
9. Take complete ownership of your project execution, delivery, and quality.
10. Start seeing confusion, discomfort, and errors as signs of progress (not failure).
11. Maintain confident body language stand tall, sit straight.
12. Stay enthusiastic and keep a smile especially during challenges.
13. Understand users deeply think from their point of view when coding.
14. Pursue mastery until tasks become second nature (can solve even half-asleep).
15. Never stop coding until your work meets this checklist:
		i. Make it work
		ii Make it readable
		iii Make it modular
		iv Make it efficient
