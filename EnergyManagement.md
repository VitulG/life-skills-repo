# When do you find getting into the Stress quadrant?
1. When deadlines are very tight -> I feel stressed when deadlines are extremely tight and unexpected work gets added. In such cases, I communicate early, reorganize tasks, and stay focused on the most impactful items.
2. When I try to do everything myself -> I feel stressed if I avoid delegation or take on more than necessary. I’ve learned to ask for help, collaborate, and distribute tasks better.
3. When I work too long without breaks -> If I keep working continuously without short breaks, stress builds up. Now I manage this by taking quick mindful pauses to reset.

# How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?
1. When I feel highly motivated and time passes quickly -> I know I’m in the Excitement Quadrant when I’m so engaged that I don’t notice time passing. I feel energetic and look forward to completing the task.
2. When I generate new ideas or solve problems creatively -> I understand I’m in the Excitement Quadrant when I start coming up with new ideas, solutions and feel mentally stimulated.
3. When I voluntarily take initiative -> I notice I’m excited when I take initiative without being asked and feel eager to contribute more.
